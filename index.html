About the Project

Plankton plays a crucial role in assessing the health of aquatic ecosystems, responding quickly to environmental changes. Leveraging AI and high-speed imaging, this project seeks to improve plankton monitoring through data from the Plankton Imaging (Pi-10) instrument aboard the RV Cefas Endeavour.

Building on the Turing Data Study Groupâ€™s (DSG) work, our project aims to enhance the RAPID toolset for real-time plankton classification. The RAPID software, deployed within six months, operates on the RV Cefas Endeavour and can classify a wide variety of marine species and objects. Ongoing developments include a real-time dashboard to provide continuous plankton metrics and explainable AI (xAI) to enhance transparency and collaboration with ecologists.

To improve classification accuracy, we introduce **ViT-LASNet**, a framework combining Vision Transformers (ViT), BEiT models, and dynamic Label-Aware Smoothing (LAS) to enhance real-time plankton classification on the Pi-10 dataset.

Key Contributions

  • Adoption of ViT and BEiT models for superior image classification performance
  • Integration of dynamic label-aware smoothing to address long-tail data challenges
  • Effective use of transfer learning to improve performance on the newly introduced Pi-10 dataset
